### Screenshot Design Guidelines

- Place captions at the **top** of each screenshot (best OCR detection)
- Use **high-contrast text** on solid or semi-transparent backgrounds
- Avoid decorative or script fonts (OCR may not read them)
- Text should occupy **no more than 20%** of the screenshot area
- Show actual app UI below the caption (not just marketing graphics)
- Design for the **smallest display size** (text must be legible on iPhone SE)
- Use the app's actual color scheme for consistency

## 8. Rating Prompt Strategy

### When to Trigger

**Positive moments (DO prompt):**
- After successfully completing a session within their personal limit
- After a streak of 3+ sessions within limits
- After viewing positive statistics trends
- After the 10th drink logged (engagement milestone)

**Negative moments (NEVER prompt):**
- When the user has exceeded their limit
- During an active drinking session
- On first app open
- Immediately after onboarding

### Frequency

- Maximum **3 times per year** (Apple guideline)
- Minimum **2 weeks between prompts**
- Only after at least **3 completed sessions**

### Impact Data

- Apps below 4.0 stars are often ignored
- Moving from 3.6 to 4.2 stars: **~60% higher conversion**
- Over 80% of users check at least one review before installing
- Target: **4.5+ stars**

## 11. Scientific Foundation for Marketing Claims

> These are claims that can be made in marketing materials, backed by published research.

### PRIMARY MARKETING STUDY (Used in Description Opening)

**"Self-monitoring is the #1 evidence-based technique for drinking less"**

- **Source:** Garnett, C., et al. (2015). "Behaviour change techniques used in digital self-help interventions for hazardous and harmful alcohol use — a meta-analysis." *Journal of Medical Internet Research (JMIR)*, 17(9), e220.
- **What it found:** Self-monitoring of alcohol consumption was ranked the single most effective behavior change technique (BCT) for reducing alcohol use, by a consensus panel of 50+ international experts.
- **Link:** [JMIR](https://www.jmir.org/2015/9/e220/)
- **Used in:** App Store description opening, Promotional Text, website hero section
- **Why this study:** It is a peer-reviewed expert-consensus study in a top-tier journal. The claim is accurate, specific ("ranked by 50 experts") and directly maps to GlassCount's core feature (self-monitoring). Unlike the incorrectly-cited Annals of Behavioral Medicine study (which covers primary-care screening, not digital self-monitoring), this study is an exact match for the claim.

### SUPPORTING STUDY (Confirms mechanism at scale)

**"Self-monitoring mediates alcohol reduction"**

- **Source:** Oldham, M., Garnett, C., et al. (2024). "The role of self-monitoring in the effectiveness of a digital alcohol intervention." *The Lancet Regional Health – Europe*, 37, 100803.
- **What it found:** In a study of 5,602 participants, self-monitoring was confirmed as the active mechanism through which a digital intervention reduced alcohol consumption. Participants who consistently self-monitored showed the greatest reductions.
- **Link:** [The Lancet Regional Health – Europe](https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lanepe/article/PIIS2666-7762(23)00247-5/fulltext)
- **Used in:** Press kit, website, investor deck — adds large-scale validation to primary study

### All Claims We CAN Make

| Claim | Source | How to Use | Priority |
|-------|--------|-----------|----------|
| "Self-monitoring is the #1 evidence-based technique for drinking less" | Garnett et al. 2015, JMIR (50-expert consensus) | **Description opening, Promotional Text, hero messaging** | **PRIMARY** |
| "Self-monitoring mediates alcohol reduction at scale" | Oldham/Garnett et al. 2024, The Lancet (5,602 participants) | Press kit, website, investor deck | High |

| "Setting personal limits increases self-efficacy and reduces consumption" | ResearchGate study on self-monitoring and goal-setting | Limit feature marketing | Medium |
| "Users of tracking + goal-setting apps drink up to 33% less" | Sunnyside efficacy study (46K users) | General category claim (not specific to GlassCount) | Medium |
| "49% of Americans are trying to drink less" | Circana 2025 survey | Market context, "you're not alone" messaging | Low |

### Claims We CANNOT Make

| Claim | Why Not |
|-------|---------|
| "GlassCount will help you drink X% less" | No GlassCount-specific efficacy data yet |
| "Safe to drive at this level" | Explicitly prohibited in CLAUDE.md and legally dangerous |

| "BAC" in title or subtitle | User requirement -- restricted to keyword field only |
| "Accurate BAC reading" | It's an estimate, always label as such |

## 12. ASO Rules & Character Limits

### Character Limits Reference

| Field | Apple App Store | Google Play Store |
|-------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Title** | 30 characters | 30 characters |
| **Subtitle / Short Description** | 30 characters (subtitle) | 80 characters (short description) |
| **Keyword Field** | 100 characters | N/A (no keyword field) |
| **Promotional Text** | 170 characters | N/A |
| **Long Description** | 4,000 characters | 4,000 characters |
| **What's New** | 4,000 characters | 500 characters |
| **Screenshots** | Up to 10 | Up to 8 |
| **App Preview Videos** | Up to 3 (30 sec each) | 1 (30s - 2min, YouTube) |

### Indexing Differences

| Field | Apple (Indexed?) | Google Play (Indexed?) |
|-------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Title | **YES** | **YES** |
| Subtitle / Short Description | **YES** | **YES** |
| Keyword Field | **YES** | N/A |
| Long Description | **NO** | **YES** |
| Promotional Text | **NO** | N/A |
| Screenshot Captions | **YES (new 2025)** | NO |
| In-App Purchase Names | YES | N/A |
| Developer Name | YES | YES |

### Apple-Specific Rules

1. **Never repeat keywords** across Title + Subtitle + Keyword Field (counts once regardless)
2. **DO repeat keywords** in screenshot captions (separate signal, new 2025)
3. **Keyword field format:** Comma-separated, NO spaces: `BAC,calculator,blood`
4. **Singular OR plural**, not both (Apple understands both from one entry)
5. **Fill all 100 characters** of keyword field
6. **In-App Purchase display names** are indexed -- name them with keywords

### Google Play-Specific Rules

1. **Long description IS fully indexed** -- target 2-3% keyword density
2. **First and last 180 characters** of long description carry extra weight
3. **Short description** is second most important field
4. **Repeating keywords** from title in short description CAN help (unlike Apple)
5. **Google uses NLP** -- semantic relationships and natural language matter
6. **Crash rates** and stability metrics are ranking factors

### Things That Will Get Rejected

- Trademarked competitor names in metadata
- "#1" or "best" claims without substantiation
- "Free" in the title (if the app has IAP)
- Keyword-stuffed titles or subtitles (must read naturally)
- Misleading screenshots that don't match the app
- Medical claims without proper regulatory approval
